### **Key Strategy Refinements:**
1.  **Remove In-Text Pricing**: Mentioning "25,000 KRW" in the post title or main body often flags the post as "Commercial." Leave the pricing for the private KakaoTalk/SMS conversation.
2.  **Use "공유" (Share) instead of "판매" (Seller)**: Language like "Sharing a spot" (자리를 나눕니다) or "Splitting costs" (N빵) aligns better with the user-to-user sharing culture.
3.  **Frame it as "Empty Spots"**: Use words like "Vacant spot" (빈 자리) or "Joint purchase" (공구).

### **Key Strategy for "Non-Commercial" Posts:**
1.  **Remove All Styling**: No HTML, colors, or bold tags. Plain text is the standard for non-business users.
2.  **Focus on "Cost Sharing" (N-bbang)**: Frame the message as looking for a "family member" to split costs rather than Selling a product.
3.  **Soft Keywords**: Use terms like "가족 멤버 모집" (Recruiting family members) or "빈 자리 공유" (Sharing empty slots) instead of "판매" (Sale).
4.  **Implicit Trust**: Mentioning you have a business registration is fine as a trust signal, but keep it brief so it doesn't look like a corporate ad.
